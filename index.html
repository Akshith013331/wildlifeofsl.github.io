WILDLIFE


of Sri Lanka



Sri Lanka's biodiversity thrives in its varied landscapes, from dense rainforests to arid plains. Home to iconic species like elephants and leopards, its national parks, including Yala and Wilpattu, showcase a vibrant array of wildlife. This island nation remains a captivating sanctuary for unique and endangered flora and fauna.


BIODIVERSITY

Sri Lanka is considered a biodiversity hotspot due to its high concentration of endemic species. The island's isolation and varied climate have led to the evolution of unique flora and fauna found nowhere else in the world.

Flora and fauna collectively refer to the plant and animal life in a specific region or environment. Flora encompasses the diverse plant species, while fauna encompasses the animal species present.

FLORA

Sri Lanka's lush vegetation comprises over 3,000 flowering plant species, including orchids, jasmine, and medicinal plants.

The country's rainforests, such as Sinharaja Forest Reserve, showcase an array of endemic trees like ironwood and satinwood. The revered Ceylon cinnamon and vibrant flowers like the Nymphaea stellata add to the nation's botanical allure.

FAUNA

Sri Lanka is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Its tropical climate sustains a diverse range of animals, including Asian elephants, leopards, and sloth bears. The island is home to rare and endemic species like the Sri Lankan leopard and the purple-faced langur.

The avian population is equally impressive, with over 430 bird species, such as the Sri Lankan junglefowl and the vibrant Sri Lanka blue magpie. Coastal areas teem with marine life, including dolphins, whales, and sea turtles that nest along Sri Lanka's sandy shores.
